SPECIAL BULLETIN — JULY 22, 2025

MILESTONE TRAGEDY: 21/07 – A Date We’ll Never Forget

Uttara, Diabari — What began as a regular school day at Milestone School & College turned into a nightmare. At approximately midday on July 21, an FT-7 fighter jet crashed into the educational building, erupting into flames that devoured classrooms, shattered lives, and left behind charred futures.

The pilot, Towkir, reportedly lost control mid-air. What followed was not just a mechanical failure — it was a collapse of humanity, of responsibility, of justice.

Burned by Fire, Betrayed by System

As students screamed and bodies were carried out, help was neither fast nor free. Local rickshaw-pullers, CNG drivers — once the lifeline of our streets — raised fares from 20 to 200… 50 to 1000. Two-liter water bottles were sold at 600 taka near the site. People ran with children, not for help, but for profit.

Rescue operations were delayed. Bodies were recovered late at night — not at the “officially declared” 8PM deadline, but at 3AM. Survivors say soldiers were blocked, firemen stood alone, and false news spread faster than fire itself.

Cover-Ups and Cold Hearts

Authorities initially claimed “all students rescued.” But the next morning, more bodies were pulled out. Even now, the numbers don’t add up. The official death toll? 22. The unofficial number whispered in hospital morgues? 200+.

Why were we lied to? Why were bodies hidden from cameras? Why were we made to believe it was “under control”? And most of all — why is there no apology?

What We Saw

We saw children burn. We saw mothers faint. We saw uniformed men struggle in silence — and some, stay silent by choice. We saw opportunists make profit off pain. We saw a tragedy become a stage for shame.

In Memoriam

“A nation that loses its children, loses its future.”

We mourn. We remember. We demand answers. Let this not be a headline today and forgotten tomorrow. We call upon the Bangladesh Air Force, the Army, and civil authorities to answer — not just to us, but to the souls they could not save.
